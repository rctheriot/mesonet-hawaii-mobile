## 1. What Is the Hawaiʻi Mesonet App?

The **Hawaiʻi Mesonet** is a statewide network of weather stations spread across the Hawaiian Islands. Each station continuously measures things like temperature, rainfall, wind, humidity, and soil conditions.

This app lets you:

- **See every station** on a map of Hawaiʻi.
- **Check the latest weather** at any station, updated throughout the day.
- **Look back in time** with simple charts showing how conditions changed over hours or days.
- **Save your favorite stations** so the ones you care about are always one tap away.

Everything in the app is **free to view** and **read-only** — you are looking at live data, and nothing you tap will change or break anything. Feel free to explore.

## 2. Getting Around: The Two Main Buttons

At the **bottom of the screen** you will always see two buttons. These are the two main areas of the app:

| Button | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **My Stations** (bookmark icon) | Only the stations *you* have saved as favorites. |
| **Station Network** (broadcast-tower icon) | *Every* station in the Hawaiʻi Mesonet. |

Tap either button to switch between the two areas at any time.

At the **top-right of the screen**, on every main page, you will also find two small round buttons:

- The **gear icon** opens **Settings** (light/dark appearance and units).
- The **question-mark icon (?)** opens the in-app **Help** window, which includes a glossary of weather terms.

> **📷 FIGURE 3 — Navigation buttons**
> *Suggested image: The bottom of the screen with the "My Stations" and "Station Network" buttons labeled, plus a callout pointing to the gear and "?" buttons at the top-right.*

> **💡 Tip:** When you first open the app, you will land on **My Stations**. If you have not saved anything yet, it will invite you to start exploring. We will cover that in [Section 6](#6-my-stations-saving-your-favorites).

## 3. Understanding Station Colors

Throughout the app, each station has a **status** shown by a colored dot. The colors mean:

| Color | Status | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 **Green** | Active | The station is online and reporting data normally. |
| 🔴 **Red** | Inactive | The station is currently offline or not reporting. |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | Planned | The station is planned for the future but not yet running. |
| ⚪ **Gray** | Unknown | The status could not be determined. |

You will also sometimes see a small **amber "Stale Data"** note on a station's page. This means the station is technically active, but its most recent reading is more than 24 hours old — so treat those numbers with a little caution.

## 4. Station Network: Browsing All Stations

Tap **Station Network** at the bottom of the screen to see every station in Hawaiʻi. You can view them two ways — as a **Map** or as a **List**. Use the **Map / List** toggle at the top-center of the screen to switch.

> **📷 FIGURE 5 — Map/List toggle**
> *Suggested image: The top bar of the Station Network screen with the "Map" and "List" toggle highlighted.*

### 4.1 The Map View

The map shows the Hawaiian Islands with a colored dot for each station.

- **Move around:** Drag with one finger to pan. Pinch with two fingers to zoom in and out.
- **Find yourself:** Tap the **crosshair button** (the circle-with-lines icon near the map) to center the map on your current location. The first time you do this, your phone will ask permission to use your location — see [Section 8](#8-using-your-location).
- **Open a station:** Tap any dot to open that station's full details. (See [Section 5](#5-looking-at-a-single-station).)

> **📷 FIGURE 6 — Map view with crosshair**
> *Suggested image: The map full of station dots, with the crosshair "center on my location" button circled.*

> **💡 Tip:** For privacy, station dots are shown at their approximate location, nudged very slightly from the exact installation spot. Distances and directions in the app are still accurate.

### 4.2 Coloring the Map by Weather

Just below the top bar is a **"Showing:"** dropdown menu. This is one of the most useful features in the app. It lets you **color every station by a single weather measurement**, so you can compare conditions across the islands at a glance.

Tap the dropdown and choose what you want to see:

- **Status** (the default) — colors by online/offline status, as described in [Section 3](#3-understanding-station-colors).
- **Air Temp** — air temperature
- **Humidity** — how moist the air is
- **Radiation** — incoming sunlight
- **Rainfall (24hr)** — rain totaled over the last 24 hours
- **Soil Moisture** — how wet the ground is
- **Soil Temp** — ground temperature
- **Wind** — wind speed (with little arrows showing direction)

When you pick a weather measurement, each station shows a small colored label with its current value, and a **color scale legend** appears in the bottom-left corner so you know what the colors mean (for example, blue = cooler, red = warmer).

The little **information icon (ⓘ)** next to the dropdown explains the measurement you have selected in plain language.

> **📷 FIGURE 7 — Map colored by Air Temp**
> *Suggested image: The map with "Air Temp" selected in the "Showing" dropdown, stations colored blue-to-red, and the color legend visible in the bottom-left corner.*

### 4.3 The List View

Tap **List** in the top toggle to see all stations as a scrollable list instead of a map. Each row shows:

- A **colored status dot** on the left.
- The **station name**.
- The **island** it is on, and your **distance** to it (if you have shared your location).
- The selected **measurement value** (or status) on the right.

At the top of the list are three controls:

1. **Saved** — tap to show only the stations you have favorited.
2. **Island** — filter the list down to a single island (for example, only Oʻahu).
3. **Sort** — choose the order of the list:
   - **A–Z** — alphabetical by name.
   - **Distance** — nearest to you first (requires your location).
   - **By Value** — highest measurement value first.

> **📷 FIGURE 8 — List view with filters**
> *Suggested image: The Station Network list with the "Saved," island, and "Sort" controls labeled at the top, and a few station rows showing name, island · distance, and a value.*

> **💡 Tip:** Tap any row to open that station's full details, exactly like tapping a dot on the map.

## 5. Looking at a Single Station

Tap any station — on the map or in the list — to open its **detail page**. This is where you see everything about that one station.

At the very top you will find a **Back** button (to return to where you came from), plus the **gear** and **?** buttons.

> **📷 FIGURE 9 — Station detail overview**
> *Suggested image: A full station detail page with the big reading at top, the Save button, and the three tabs (Readings, Location, Info) labeled.*

### 5.1 The Big Reading at the Top

The top of the page (the "hero") shows the station's name and one **large, easy-to-read measurement** — by default, the air temperature.

Just under the name is a quick status line showing:

- The station's **status** (Active, Inactive, etc.).
- The **island**.
- **When it last updated** (for example, "Updated 5m ago").
- A **"Stale Data"** note in amber if the latest reading is over 24 hours old.

If you select two measurements in the chart below (explained in [Section 5.3](#53-the-history-chart)), the top will show **both readings side by side**.

> **📷 FIGURE 10 — The hero reading**
> *Suggested image: Close-up of the top of a station page showing the large temperature number, the station name, and the status line beneath it.*

### 5.2 The Readings Tab

The **Readings** tab (selected by default) has two parts:

1. The **History chart** (covered next).
2. **Current Readings** — a grid of every measurement the station is currently reporting, grouped into categories like Temperature, Rainfall, Wind, and Soil.

Each reading is shown on its own card. Wind speed and direction are combined into a single card with a compass heading. Rainfall is shown as a 24-hour total.

Tap the small **information icon (ⓘ)** in the corner of any reading card to get a plain-language explanation of what that measurement means.

> **📷 FIGURE 11 — Current Readings grid**
> *Suggested image: The grid of reading cards, with one card's small "ⓘ" info icon circled.*

### 5.3 The History Chart

The chart shows how a measurement has changed **over time**. This is one of the most powerful parts of the app, and it works hand-in-hand with the readings grid.

**To choose what the chart shows:**
Tap any reading card in the **Current Readings** grid below. That measurement is added to the chart and also becomes the big number at the top of the page.

**To compare two measurements:**
Tap a second reading card. The chart will plot **both at once** (for example, temperature and humidity together), each in its own color.

**To change the time span:**
Use the time-range buttons above the chart (such as **24h**) to look back over different periods.

**To start over:**
Tap **Clear** to remove all selected measurements. The top of the page will then say "Select a reading below to view it here" until you choose one.

> **📷 FIGURE 12 — History chart with two lines**
> *Suggested image: The history chart showing two colored lines, the time-range buttons above it, and the "Clear" button.*

> **💡 Tip:** Tapping a reading card that is already selected will un-select it.

### 5.4 The Location Tab

Tap the **Location** tab to see the station's spot on a small map. This is handy for understanding where the station sits relative to towns, coastlines, and terrain.

> **📷 FIGURE 13 — Location tab**
> *Suggested image: The Location tab showing a single station marker on a small map.*

### 5.5 The Info Tab

Tap the **Info** tab for background details about the station, such as its name, island, and elevation.

## 6. My Stations: Saving Your Favorites

**My Stations** is your personal shortlist. Instead of hunting through every station each time, you save the ones you care about — your neighborhood, your farm, a favorite hiking area — and they live here for quick access.

### 6.1 Saving a Station

1. Go to **Station Network** and open any station (tap it on the map or in the list).
2. At the top-right of the station's page, tap the **Save** button (bookmark icon).
3. The button changes to **Saved**, and the station is now in **My Stations**.

> **📷 FIGURE 15 — The Save button**
> *Suggested image: The top of a station page with the "Save" button highlighted, plus an inset showing it after it changes to "Saved."*

### 6.2 Viewing Your Saved Stations

Tap **My Stations** at the bottom of the screen.

- If you have **not saved anything yet**, you will see a friendly message and an **Explore Stations** button that takes you straight to the network to start saving.
- Once you have favorites, My Stations works just like the Station Network — with a **Map / List** toggle and the same **"Showing:"** measurement selector to color your saved stations by temperature, rainfall, wind, and more.

In the **List** view here, you can also sort your saved stations by **A–Z**, **By Value**, or **Distance**.

> **📷 FIGURE 16 — My Stations (empty state)**
> *Suggested image: The My Stations screen before anything is saved, showing the "You haven't added any stations yet" message and the blue "Explore Stations" button.*

> **📷 FIGURE 17 — My Stations (with saved stations)**
> *Suggested image: The My Stations list with several saved stations and the "Showing" selector at the top.*

### 6.3 Removing a Station

1. Open the station you want to remove (from My Stations or the network).
2. Tap the **Saved** button at the top-right. It toggles back to **Save**, and the station leaves your favorites.

## 7. Settings: Appearance and Units

Tap the **gear icon** at the top-right of any main screen to open **Settings**. There are two choices:

- **Appearance** — switch between **Light** and **Dark** theme. Dark is easier on the eyes at night.
- **Units** — switch between:
  - **Imperial** — °F, inches, miles per hour (common in the U.S.).
  - **Metric** — °C, millimeters, meters per second.

Your choices are remembered the next time you open the app.

## 8. Using Your Location

The app can use your phone's location for two convenient features:

- Centering the map on where you are (the **crosshair** button).
- Showing **how far away** each station is, and sorting by **Distance**.

The first time the app needs your location, your phone will ask for permission. **Allowing it is optional** — the rest of the app works fine without it; you simply won't see distances or the "center on me" feature.

**If you said no earlier and want to turn it back on:**

- **iPhone:** Settings → Safari → Location → Allow.
- **Android:** Settings → Apps → Chrome → Permissions → Location.

> **💡 Note:** Location features only work within the Hawaiian Islands. If the app sees that you are outside Hawaiʻi, it will let you know that distance sorting is unavailable.

## 9. Installing the App on Your Phone

You can add the Hawaiʻi Mesonet app to your phone's home screen so it opens full-screen, just like a regular app — no app store required.

**The easy way:**
Open the **Help** window (the **?** button), go to the **Install & Location** tab, and tap **Add to Home Screen**. On some phones this will offer to install it for you automatically.

**By hand:**

- **iPhone / iPad:** In Safari, tap the **Share** button (the square with an arrow), then choose **Add to Home Screen**.
- **Android:** In Chrome, tap the **⋮ menu** (three dots), then choose **Add to Home Screen**.

Once installed, tap its icon any time to open the app instantly.

> **📷 FIGURE 19 — Add to Home Screen**
> *Suggested image: The "Install & Location" tab of the Help window with the "Add to Home Screen" button highlighted.*

> **💡 Note:** When installed this way, the app stays in **portrait (upright)** orientation, which is how it is designed to be used.

## 10. Tips and Troubleshooting

**The screen says "Please rotate your device."**
The app is designed to be used upright (portrait). Simply turn your phone back to vertical.

**A station shows "Stale Data."**
That station's newest reading is more than 24 hours old. The numbers may not reflect current conditions.

**A station has no value / shows a dash (—).**
That station may be offline, or it may not measure the thing you selected in the "Showing" menu.

**Distances aren't showing.**
You likely haven't shared your location. See [Section 8](#8-using-your-location).

**The map looks blank or stations are missing.**
Check your internet connection. The app needs a connection to load live weather data. Try switching between Map and List, or reopening the app.

**I don't understand a weather term.**
Open the **Help** window (the **?** button) and tap the **Glossary** tab for plain-language explanations of every measurement. You can also tap the **ⓘ** icon next to any reading.
